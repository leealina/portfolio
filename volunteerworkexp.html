Las Positas Fencing Center Volunteering

I have volunteered at the Las Positas Fencing Center for about two years. I still volunteer there on Tuesdays and Thursdays when I have time. I am an assistant coach and I help beginners to competitors to improve their skills in fencing. At the club, I also create activities and drills to get the fencers into better shape. This volunteer experience is teaching me how to be patient with people. Also, I learned how to be a leader but not intimidating people.

When I have free time, I coach the fencers at local tournaments and I also get volunteer hours by doing this. I learned to keep my cool when they don't do things I tell them to do or when they lose. Overall, volunteering at the Las Positas Fencing Center taught me a lot of things that I would have never learned without it.


AFM Referee

September 2018

I worked at the AFM RYC as a referee. The Academy of Fencing Masters had a Regional Youth Circuit tournament and I was hired to referee for two days. I refereed the foil and epee events because I don't know how to ref saber. There were about 60 competitors in each event and I did parts of each event. They paid $150 per day so in all, I got $300 for two days of work.

This work experience taught me patience and how to deal with conflict. I had to be calm when coaches and fencers were yelling at me in order to avoid further problems. This was important because referees had to make the tournament run smoothly so it wouldn't get behind.
